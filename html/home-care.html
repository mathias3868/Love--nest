elderly care

Elderly care encompasses a broad range of services and support aimed at meeting the physical, emotional, and social needs of older adults. As individuals age, they may require assistance with activities of daily living, healthcare, social engagement, and emotional support.

Elderly care is dedicated support that addresses the physical and mental needs of people as they age. It can include short-term assistance to recover from a specific condition, respite care to give unpaid carers a break, or long-term support. Elderly care can include a range of services tailored to the individual and their specific situation - from helping them maintain a healthy and nutritious diet, to encouraging gentle and positive movement. Elderly care can help individuals maintain a positive quality of life during their later years.

what is the importance of Elderly care

The world is aging faster than ever before. According to the Centre for Aging Better, there are currently 11 million people aged 65 in England alone, and this is set to rise to 13 million in the next 10 years.

The ageing process does not always lead to disability, although 86 percent of people over 85 are living with at least one chronic long-term health condition. It's projected that the number of individuals in care in the UK will double by 2035, meaning care services are gaining increased importance. The use of their services enables our loved ones to maintain independence and enjoy a longer quality of life.

who would benefit from elderly care services

Elderly individuals experiencing difficulty with daily activities due health difficulties, mobility issues, or loneliness may find assistance and support through elderly care services. For example, those living with dementia, complex medical conditions like Parkinson's Disease, or finding it difficult to keep up with personal care may see their quality of life improve with professional support.

what is personal care?

Personal care refers to the assistance provided to individuals with activities related to daily living, hygiene, and grooming. It encompasses a range of tasks that help individuals maintain their personal hygiene, appearance, and overall well-being. Personal care services are often provided to those who may have difficulty performing these tasks independently due to age, disability, illness, or other factors.

A personal care can assist with:

Bathing and Showering

Assistance with bathing or showering involves helping individuals clean their bodies thoroughly, ensuring they are comfortable and safe during the process.

Toileting and Incontinence Care

This involves assisting individuals with using the toilet, maintaining continence, and managing incontinence issues if present.

Dressing and Undressing

Personal care aides help individuals select appropriate clothing, put on and take off garments, fasten buttons, tie shoelaces, and ensure clothing is comfortable and properly fitted.

Assistance and Mobility

Personal care aides may help individuals move around safely, transfer from one place to another (e.g., from bed to chair), and use mobility aids such as walkers or wheelchairs.

Medication Reminders

While personal care aides typically do not administer medications, they may remind individuals to take their medications as prescribed and assist with opening containers or organizing pillboxes.

Grooming

Grooming tasks include hair care (combing, brushing, washing, and styling), oral hygiene (brushing teeth, denture care, and mouthwash), shaving (for men), and nail care (trimming and filing nails).
